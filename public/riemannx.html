RiemannX

RiemannX collects numerical experiments around zeta zeros, prime-counting approximations, and Keiper-Li coefficients. These are diagnostics, not proof certificates.

Source Method Notes

Browser Prime Inspector

Enter an integer to test primality, find nearby primes, and count primes up to that value exactly when the browser cap allows.

What It Computes

Prime Counts

Compares exact browser-capped prime counts with the simple `n / log(n)` approximation.

Zero Diagnostics

Records numerical zeta-zero and spacing diagnostics in Python experiments where configured.

Coefficient Signals

Explores Keiper-Li-style numerical signals as search diagnostics, subject to precision limits.

Numerical diagnostics can suggest what to inspect. They do not prove the Riemann Hypothesis.
